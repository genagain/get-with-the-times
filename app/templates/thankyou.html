1909

Norway becomes the first country to provide paid maternity leave with 12 weeks total, six of which were mandatory after childbirth.

Paid family leaveis long overdue,America.

1911

Germany amends laws to provide two weeks of paid maternity leave before delivery. Women were also banned from working three weeks before birth.

1925

Chile writes 12 weeks of paid family leave into law. The unpaid leave period was also increased to six weeks

Change the times,America.

1936

Turkey enacts a law banning pregnant women from working three weeks before and after delivery, with all leave compensated at 50% of earnings.

1945

France passes a law granting 14 weeks of maternity leave paid at 50% of earnings.

Strong families areEveryone's business.

1950

Italy enacts mandatory job-protected leave for mothers at 80% of earnings. Unpaid job-protected leave also became available to women for 6 months after the mandatory period.

1963

Finland introduces two months of maternity leave in Act 364/1963; three weeks before and six weeks after childbirth.

Make AmericaFamily friendlyAgain.

1975

Iceland introduces three months leave for all working mothers following childbirth. The payment is equal to the unemployment benefit.

Strong families areEveryone's business.

1987

South Korea passes 60 day long, employment-protected, fully paid, and mandatory maternity leave. Unpaid parental leave till the child’s first birthday is also offered.

It's about time,America.

2016

The United States is the only industrialized nation that does not provide paid leave.

Along with Papua New Guinea.

2016

Only California, New Jersey, & Rhode Island have implemented laws for paid leave.

It has actually improved their economies.

2016
